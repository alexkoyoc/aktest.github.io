Hello, I am a fourth-year undergraduate student in the Department of Linguistics at UCLA.

I am currently pursuing my B.A. in Linguistics and Psychology with the intent of applying to a Ph.D. program in the upcoming application cycle. Many of my current interests revolve around psycholinguistics. I am interested in linguistic processing, predictive strategies, and the mechanisms used during language processing. Some additional areas I love to explore include bilingualism, syntax, and semantics. Broadly speaking, I enjoy looking at the cognition of linguistic representations.

I will be graduating in Spring of 2026, and have completed an Honors Thesis under the supervision of Jack Duff. Much of my time is currently spent taking linguistic courses, creating experimental stimuli, and reading relevant literature of interest. My current project is my honors thesis, which looks at cataphora resolution in native and heritage Mexican Spanish speakers. I undertook this project to further understand how comprehenders interpret pronouns and examine how structural constraints and featural information guide sentence comprehension.

Don't mind the current aesthetic, this website is still a WIP!
